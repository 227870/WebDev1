There are many different parts in a car that help it function correctly. We will cover some of the more important components briefly.

The components we will be looking at are:

  1. The Battery
  2. The Radiator
  3. The Brakes
  4. The Suspension System
  5. The Exhaust System

Some other components like the engine and transmission have their own page.

The Battery

The car battery helps start a car up by making an electric current. Car batteries have cells that contain one plate of lead and another plate of lead dioxide. When the plates are submerged by sulfuric acid, a chemical reaction occurs that provides the electricity needed to start a car.

The Radiator

The radiator's job is to cool down the engine and prevent it from getting too hot. A thermostat located near the front of the engine will detect when it's getting too hot. When this happens, coolant and water is released to pass through the engine and take the excess heat back into the radiator. The liquid is then cooled down and exchanges heat with the air outside of the car.

The Brakes

The brakes help stop the wheels of a car by creating friction between the brake rotors and the brake calipers. This is done when brake fluid is sent to the brake caliper, which causes the brake pistons to press against the brake rotor. There are two types of brakes, which are drum brakes and disc brakes. Drum brakes are usually found in heavier vehicles like trucks while disc brakes are found in less heavy cars.

The Suspension System

The suspension system's job is to dissipate forces applied against the car on the underside. It does this by turning energy into heat, which is done with springs, dampers and struts. When a car passes over a bump, the springs store the energy from the bump and dampers help control the motion of the spring. This helps to provide comfort to the cabin and help the tires stay on the road.

The Exhaust System

The exhaust system helps to take exhaust gases from the engine (more information on the engine page) and release them from the car. Gases that are output from the engine are collected by the exhaust manifold, which acts like a funnel to take all of the exhaust gases from each engine cylinders. The gases go through the catalytic converter, which helps filter the gas from harmful elements. Then, the gas passes through a silencer or muffler and are then released from the car.
