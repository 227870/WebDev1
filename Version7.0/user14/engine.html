Engines

There are quite a few different types of engines found in cars. The engines we will look at are the v engine, inline engine and boxer engine.

What do engines do?

The engine's job is to make your car move by converting gas into motion. The engines we're looking at are internal combustion engines, which usually follow the four-stroke cycle.

  1. The intake valves opens and a mixture of gasoline and air go into the combustion chamber.
  2. The valves close and the piston moves up and compresses the air and fuel.
  3. A spark plug fires to help the mixture combust, forcing the piston to move back down.
  4. The exhaust valves open and the exhaust gases are pushed out by the piston.

Note that this explanation doesn't perfectly apply to all engines, such a diesel engines for example, which doesn't need spark plugs.

The V Engine

V engines got their name because they're shaped like a V. The engine's cylinder banks are set up in a V shape creating either a 60 or 90 degree angle. The first V engine was created by Wilhelm Mayback in 1889.

The Inline Engine

Inline engines (also called straight engines) are engines where all of the pistons and cylinders are set up in a straight row. Inline engines are usually more balanced and quiter than V engines, but have a higher center of gravity.
