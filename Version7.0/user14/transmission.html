Transmissions

Many types of transmissions are found in cars. The transmissions you will learn about are the manual, automatic, sequential and dual clutch transmissions.

Hover over an image above to see which transmission it is.

What do transmissions do?

Transmissions help you control how much power is being delivered to your car so your engine isn't spinning to fast or too slow. Transmissions are needed in cars because without it, the engine would destroy itself. Transmissions have different gears and can change those gears based on your acceleration and speed.

Automatic Transmissions

Automatic transmissions don't require human input to change gears. Instead, automatic transmissions use sensors and processors to automatically shift gears. The engine is always rotating, but the torque from the engine needs to be disconnected from the transmission so it can shift properly. To solve this, automatic transmissions use torque converters to help the transmission shift correctly and prevent the engine from stalling.

Manual Transmissions

Manual transmissions require human input to shift gears. Drivers use a shift lever to affect how gears on the input shaft interact with the output shaft's gears. Drivers usually go through an H pattern to shift (shown below). Similar to how automatic transmissions use torque converters, manual transmissions use clutches to help disconnect the rotation of the engine from the transmission.

Sequential Transmissions

Sequential transmissions are a type of manual transmission. Unlike a regular manual transmission, drivers can only go forward and back to shift rather than going through an H pattern. This means you can only shift one gear up or one gear down from your current gear. These transmissions are faster than regular manual transmissions and are usually found in race cars.
